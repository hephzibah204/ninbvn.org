Nigerian Passport Services in the Diaspora

Applying for or renewing a passport from outside Nigeria can be a daunting process. Delays often occur due to incorrect fee payments, incomplete documentation, or mismatching records on the NIMC (NIN) database. Our specialized passport processing support desk helps you ensure every part of your application is perfect before you head to your biometric capturing appointment at the High Commission.

Nigerian Passport Renewal

If your passport is expired, or has less than 6 months of validity remaining, you are eligible for a renewal. Standard renewal options are available for 5-year or 10-year validity books depending on age. The key to a successful renewal is ensuring the names on your application match the names on your NIN slip exactly.

First-Time / Child Applications

For children born in the UK/Germany or adults obtaining their first Nigerian passport, a fresh application is required. In addition to standard identity checks, you must provide proof of citizenship (e.g., father's/mother's Nigerian passport or local birth certificate) and obtain a child's NIN before starting the immigration forms.

Required Documents Checklist

Ensure you gather the following documents for your passport application processing:

  • ✓ Nigerian international Passport Data Page
  • ✓ Nigerian NIN Verification Slip
  • ✓ Recent passport photograph
  • ✓ Current Signature

⚠️ Original Documents Required

You must bring the physical original of the above documents along when coming to process your Passport. Photocopies or digital soft copies will not be accepted during capturing.

How We Streamline Your Application

We do not issue passports directly (passports are strictly issued by the Nigerian Immigration Service at the High Commission). However, we solve the main bottlenecks that cause long processing delays:

  1. Data Alignment & NIN Verification

    We check that your NIN, BVN, and passport profiles align. If there is a spelling error, we process a NIN Modification first to prevent database blocks.

  2. Application and Payment Guidance

    We assist in navigating the NIS payment portal to ensure correct processing fees are paid without transaction drops.

  3. Embassy Appointment Scheduling

    We help secure your capturing date, guiding you through what to expect on the day of your visit to the High Commission.

Frequently Asked Questions

Generally, once biometric capture is completed at the Nigerian High Commission, standard passport booklet production takes between 6 to 12 weeks. Fast-track options may be available depending on embassy capacity.

For lost passports, you will need a police report documenting the loss, an official fee payment for a lost passport replacement, and your NIN slip. We can guide you through gathering these specific requirements before you book your capturing appointment.
