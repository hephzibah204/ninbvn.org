What is a BVN?

Key facts

  • BVN (Bank Verification Number) is an 11-digit biometric identifier used across all Nigerian banks.
  • Protects against identity theft and fraud, and is required to operate any Nigerian bank account.
  • You only have one BVN — it stays with you across all banks for life.

The Bank Verification Number system was introduced by the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) to curb fraud and protect customers. Without a BVN, you cannot operate a Nigerian bank account. Our trained staff make the capture process quick and comfortable at one of our centres.

Documents Required

For BVN Enrolment:
  • ✓ Original copy of Nigerian International Passport
  • ✓ NIMC National Identification Number (NIN) Verification Slip
For Opening a Nigerian Bank Account:
  • ✓ Your generated BVN
  • ✓ NIMC National Identification Number (NIN) Verification Slip

⚠️ Physical Documents Required

To capture, you must bring along your original valid ID card. Photocopies, scans, or digital soft copies will not be accepted for biometric validation.

How to Enrol — Step by Step

  1. Book your appointment — Call or WhatsApp +447459 788558 to schedule.
  2. Download & complete the form — Get the BVN form from our Resources page and fill it in before attending.
  3. Visit a centre — Attend your appointment with your ID and completed form.
  4. Biometric capture — Fingerprints and a facial photo are taken by our trained staff.
  5. Receive your slip — You'll get an acknowledgement slip with your transaction ID.
  6. Receive your BVN — Within 72 hours the system confirms your application and you receive an SMS with your BVN.

FAQs

You can enrol for a BVN even if you are about to open a new account. The BVN will then be linked to your account once it is opened.

Contact us and we can help you retrieve your existing BVN. You do not need to re-enrol if you are already registered.
