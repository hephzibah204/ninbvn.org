What is a NIN?

Key facts

  • The National Identity Number (NIN) is an 11-digit unique identifier issued by NIMC — the National Identity Management Commission of Nigeria.
  • It is your permanent, lifelong identity token as a Nigerian citizen. It does not expire and cannot be reassigned.
  • It links your biometric data (fingerprints, facial image) to your personal record in the Nigerian national identity database.
  • NINBVN.org is an officially licensed NIMC enrolment partner via FUWA Agribusiness, authorised to capture diaspora NIN applications across the UK and Germany.

Whether you are enrolling for the first time, recovering a lost NIN, correcting an error, or registering a child, our trained staff guide you through every step — in full compliance with NIMC standards. You do not need to travel to Nigeria.

Step-by-Step Enrolment Process

A simple, transparent process — from pre-enrolment to NIN issuance.

  1. Complete your NIMC Pre-Enrolment online

    Visit penrol.nimc.gov.ng to fill your pre-enrolment form. All mandatory fields must be completed accurately. Print your pre-enrolment slip with its 2D barcode — you must bring this to your appointment. If you are unable to pre-enrol online, our team can assist you on the day.

  2. Book your appointment (at least 24 hours in advance)

    Call or WhatsApp +447450 444858 to reserve your slot at your nearest centre. Walk-ins are not guaranteed — booking ahead is strongly recommended. You can also book multiple family members in one visit.

  3. Document review & preparation

    Our team reviews your documents ahead of or on arrival to flag any issues that could cause a rejection. We check your pre-enrolment form, identity documents and supporting materials — so you submit with confidence, not guesswork.

  4. Visit your nearest centre with all originals

    Attend your appointment with your pre-enrolment slip, all original documents, and your payment. Our enrolment officer will verify your details, scan your documents, and confirm your data before biometric capture begins.

  5. Biometric capture (~15–30 minutes)

    Our officer captures your fingerprints, facial photograph and other biometric data using NIMC-approved equipment. Data is transmitted directly and securely to the NIMC national identity database. Your personal information is not stored locally after processing.

  6. Receive your Transaction ID Slip

    After successful capture, you will receive a Transaction ID Slip as confirmation. Keep this safe — you will need it to collect your NIN slip if it is not issued on the same day.

  7. NIN issued

    Your NIN slip may be issued on the day (subject to NIMC processing) or within a few working days. NIMC will also send your NIN via SMS to the mobile number registered during enrolment. If you opted for a NIN PVC card, delivery takes approximately 10 working days.

How Long Does NIN Registration Take?

Many people ask how long does it take to get a NIN after registration — here is a realistic, honest timeline:

  1. On the day — 15 to 30 minutes

    The biometric capture itself takes 15–30 minutes once you are in the chair. Add 10–15 minutes for document verification on arrival. Total time at the centre: allow 45–60 minutes to be comfortable.

  2. Transaction ID Slip — issued same day

    You leave with a Transaction ID Slip as proof of successful enrolment. This is your reference for follow-up and can itself be used as interim proof of identity in some situations.

  3. NIN via SMS — within 1 to 5 working days

    NIMC sends your 11-digit NIN to the mobile number you registered during enrolment. This typically arrives within 1–5 working days for diaspora applications processed through FUWA Agribusiness. Some applicants receive it within hours.

  4. NIN Slip (paper) — same day or within 5 working days

    The printed NIN slip may be issued on the same day or within a few working days depending on NIMC's processing queue at the time of your appointment.

  5. NIN PVC Card (optional) — approximately 10 working days

    If you choose the physical NIN PVC card, NIMC processes and dispatches it within approximately 10 working days. This is separate from your NIN number which arrives much sooner.

⚠️ What causes delays?

  • Duplicate biometric records — if you were enrolled previously under a different name or details, the system may flag a conflict. We follow up with NIMC on your behalf if this happens.
  • Wrong phone number at pre-enrolment — your NIN SMS goes to the number in your NIMC pre-enrolment form. Double-check it before your appointment.
  • NIMC processing backlogs — during peak periods (ahead of government deadlines), NIMC's processing queue may be longer. Apply early to avoid urgency.

Is NIN Registration Closing? Deadlines You Need to Know

Many people search "when is NIN registration closing" after hearing about NIMC deadlines in the news. Here is the current situation as we understand it:

⚠️ NIN Is Now Mandatory — Act Without Delay

  • The Nigerian government has made NIN a legal requirement for all citizens, both resident and diaspora.
  • NIN is required for passport applications and renewals — you cannot get or renew a Nigerian passport without a NIN as of the current policy.
  • NIN is required for BVN linkage — banks have been directed to restrict accounts not linked to a NIN.
  • NIN is required for SIM registration in Nigeria — unlinked SIMs face deactivation.
  • NIN is mandatory for JAMB, NYSC, voter registration, driver's licence and most government services in Nigeria.

While NIMC has not announced a hard global closure date for NIN registration itself, every deadline attached to NIN-dependent services is effectively a NIN deadline. If you do not have your NIN before your next passport renewal, your passport application will be blocked. If your bank account is flagged without a linked NIN, access to your funds will be restricted.

The practical advice is simple: register now. There is no benefit to waiting. Appointments can be booked quickly and the process is completed in a single visit.

NIN Registration for Nigerians in the UK Diaspora

The NIN registration process for Nigerians living abroad — including in the UK, Germany, USA, Canada and Australia — is specifically managed through NIMC's diaspora enrolment programme, operated via authorised partners like FUWA Agribusiness.

How Diaspora NIN Registration Differs from Nigeria-Based Registration

  • → You do not visit a NIMC state office — you attend one of our licensed UK centres
  • → The process requires an Authentication Letter from the Nigerian High Commission in London (or Embassy in Germany) — this verifies your diaspora status
  • → Data is transmitted to NIMC's national database via the FUWA Agribusiness secure portal — your NIN is fully valid and identical to one obtained in Nigeria
  • → NIN is issued via SMS by NIMC within 1–5 working days, same as in Nigeria

Getting Your High Commission Authentication Letter

Before your NIN registration appointment, you will need an Authentication / Attestation Letter from the Nigerian High Commission in London (1 Northumberland Avenue, WC2N 5BW) or the Nigerian Embassy in Berlin (Germany applicants). This letter confirms your identity and diaspora status for NIMC purposes.

  • ✓ Book an appointment at the Nigerian High Commission separately
  • ✓ Bring your Nigerian passport and UK residence proof
  • ✓ The letter is typically issued on the day or within a few days
  • ✓ Bring the original letter to your NINBVN.org NIN appointment

Not sure how to get this letter? WhatsApp us — we guide clients through this step regularly.
