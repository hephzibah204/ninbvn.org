Lost BVN Search & Retrieval for Diaspora Accounts

Your Bank Verification Number (BVN) is an 11-digit security code that links all your bank accounts in Nigeria. Without your BVN, you cannot access online banking, transfer funds, or renew bank cards. If you have lost or forgotten your BVN, do not attempt to register for a new one. Creating a duplicate BVN is blocked by the Nigeria Inter-Bank Settlement System (NIBSS) and can lead to your financial profile being flagged for investigation.

To safely retrieve your BVN, you must execute a database search. NIN-BVN Services assists diaspora customers in searching the NIBSS central index, matching biological information and previous account records to restore access to your number.

How to Check Your BVN via USSD

Standard Mobile Check (Nigerian Networks):

If you still have the phone number linked to your bank account, dial *565*0# on your mobile phone. Your 11-digit BVN will display on your screen (costs approximately N20 per inquiry).

This code works across major telecom providers in Nigeria including MTN, Airtel, Glo, and 9mobile. If you are abroad and your SIM card is roaming, make sure you have active network reception and airtime.

Lost SIM or Changed Phone Numbers?

Many diaspora bank account holders no longer use the phone number originally registered during their BVN capture. If your SIM card is lost, expired, or deactivated, the *565*0# USSD code will not work.

In this case, our agents can initiate a manual database look-up on the NIBSS platform using your verified biographical profile. Once retrieved, we can assist in updating your contact records and linking your retrieved BVN directly with your NIN.

Linking Your BVN with NIN

Under current Central Bank of Nigeria mandates, retrieving your BVN is only the first step. You must also link it to a validated National Identification Number (NIN). If the name, date of birth, or phone number on your NIN does not match the details on your BVN profile, your bank will block the linkage and freeze your account.

NIN-BVN Services provides a complete mapping, retrieval, and correction service to align your BVN and NIN records seamlessly, preventing service disruptions.

Verification Documents Required

To comply with bank security mandates and data protection laws, you must present valid proof of identity to request a BVN retrieval search:

  • ✓ Copy of your Nigerian International Passport (valid or expired)
  • ✓ Your 11-digit National Identification Number (NIN) slip
  • ✓ Name of the bank and the account number linked to your BVN
  • ✓ Basic biographical details (full name, date of birth)
