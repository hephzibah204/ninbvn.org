What Is NIN Modification?

Key Facts

  • NIN Modification lets you correct or update your existing NIN record in the NIMC national identity database.
  • Common reasons: name misspelling, wrong date of birth, incorrect phone number, or outdated address.
  • Modifications are processed via FUWA Agribusiness, our licensed NIMC partner.
  • You do not need to travel to Nigeria — we handle everything from the UK.

Your NIN is a permanent, lifelong record. Errors on it can cause problems when applying for passports, linking your BVN, or accessing government services. The sooner they are corrected, the better.

Common Errors We Fix

  • → Name misspelling or wrong surname
  • → Incorrect date of birth captured during original enrolment
  • → Wrong phone number or email address linked to NIN
  • → Outdated home address on record
  • → Gender incorrectly recorded
  • → Duplicate biometric data causing processing delays

Documents Required for Modification

Please bring originals only. Our officer will scan and return them at your appointment.

  • ✓ Your original NIN slip or NIN PVC card
  • ✓ Nigerian International Passport (valid or expired)
  • ✓ Nigerian Birth Certificate (NPC-issued)
  • ✓ Supporting document for the correction (e.g. deed poll for name change)
  • ✓ Your Transaction ID from original enrolment (if available)

Modification Process

  1. Contact us with your details

    Call or WhatsApp +447450 444858 and describe the error on your NIN record. Have your Transaction ID and NIN number ready if possible.

  2. Document review

    We confirm which supporting documents you need to bring for the specific type of correction. This step prevents rejections on the day.

  3. Book your appointment

    We schedule a slot at your nearest centre. Modification appointments often take longer than new enrolments — allow 30–45 minutes.

  4. Visit the centre

    Attend with all original documents. Our officer submits the modification request through the FUWA Agribusiness NIMC portal.

  5. Confirmation & follow-up

    You receive a modification reference number. Updates are reflected in the NIMC system within a few working days. We follow up on your behalf if needed.
