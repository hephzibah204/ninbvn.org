How to Search for a Lost NIN & Print Your Slip

Losing your National Identification Number (NIN) can cause immediate issues with passport renewals, bank accounts, and mobile lines. If you already enrolled but misplaced your NIN slip, you do not need to register again. In fact, attempting to register a second time is illegal and will result in a double-enrolment block on the NIMC database.

Instead, you must run an official search query to retrieve your existing NIN details. NIN-BVN Services provides direct database integration to securely pull up your profile using your biometrics, demographics, or previous tracking details.

Self-Service Recovery Options (For Local SIMs)

Try these methods first if you have your registered SIM:

  • USSD Retrieval Code: Dial *346# on the mobile number linked to your NIN. Select option 1 to retrieve your number (requires minimum N20 credit).
  • NIMC Mobile App: Download the MWS NIMC app and input your phone number. If it matches your record, your profile and slip will sync.

Note: If you reside in the diaspora (UK, US, Germany, etc.), your registered Nigerian phone number may be inactive or expired. In this case, USSD codes will not work, and you must use our dedicated retrieval desk.
