What is a Nigerian NIN?

Your National Identity Number (NIN) is an 11-digit unique identifier issued by the National Identity Management Commission (NIMC). It is permanent, lifelong, and cannot be reassigned. It links your fingerprints and facial photograph to your personal record in Nigeria's national identity database.

Your NIN is now required for virtually every major transaction in Nigeria — passport applications, banking, SIM registration, JAMB, NYSC, driver's licence, voter registration and more.

Step 1: Complete NIMC Pre-Enrolment Online

Before attending your appointment, visit penrol.nimc.gov.ng and complete the pre-enrolment form. All fields must be accurate — check your date of birth, name spelling and contact details carefully, as errors here end up on your NIN record.

Print your pre-enrolment slip and bring it to your appointment — it contains a 2D barcode our officer scans on the day. If you can't complete pre-enrolment online, our team can assist you when you arrive.

Step 2: Book Your Appointment

Call or WhatsApp +447459 788558 at least 24 hours in advance to reserve your slot at your nearest centre. Walk-ins are not guaranteed. If you're bringing family members, let us know how many people are coming so we can allow the right time.

Step 3: Attend Your Appointment

Arrive a few minutes early. Our officer will verify your documents, scan them, and confirm your pre-enrolment data before biometric capture begins. The capture itself — fingerprints and facial photograph — takes approximately 15–30 minutes.

After capture, you receive a Transaction ID Slip as confirmation. Keep this safe — you'll need it to follow up if your NIN doesn't arrive.

Step 4: Receive Your NIN

NIMC sends your NIN via SMS to the mobile number registered during enrolment — usually within a few working days. Your NIN slip may be issued on the day or within the same timeframe.

If you opted for a NIN PVC card, delivery takes approximately 10 working days.
