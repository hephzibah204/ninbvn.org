What is NIN Migration?

NIMC's database system has evolved over several iterations. "Migration" refers to pulling historical, archive, or pre-digital identification records (such as early-stage 2012–2014 paper card registrations, or disconnected local government area offline registries) and uploading them securely to the active, live NIMC Harmonized Database Portal.

When is Migration Required?

You will need a record migration if:

  • ✓ You registered for your identity card years ago, but the tracking code returns "Record Not Found" online.
  • ✓ Your profile is recorded on local offline servers in Nigeria but does not appear on the central database used by foreign missions and diaspora portals.
  • ✓ Your SIM card verification checks (NIN-SIM linkage) fail due to data mismatch errors between network providers and the NIMC active portal.

The Migration Pipeline

  1. 1. Document Verification

    We verify your historical registration slip, birth attestation papers, and current passports to match names and database markers.

  2. 2. Archive Query

    Our backend operators execute search commands on legacy archive systems to locate and extract your biometric hashes and demographics.

  3. 3. Digital Upload & Harmonization

    The extracted record is uploaded directly into the modern live database. A synchronized and active digital NIN slip is generated and delivered to you.

Frequently Asked Questions

If your legacy biometrics are successfully retrieved from the archives and meet current resolution specifications, they are migrated automatically. If the biometrics are damaged or missing, we will book a short biometric recapture session at our local center.
