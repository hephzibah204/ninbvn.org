Document Notarisation & Attestation Services

When presenting documents generated in the UK or Germany (such as birth certificates, corporate powers of attorney, or academic transcripts) to banks, registries, or courts in Nigeria, they will be rejected unless they are officially legalised. This process confirms the authenticity of the signature, seal, or stamp on the document so it is recognized as valid in Nigeria.

Commonly Legalised Documents

We guide clients through the attestation of several critical document types:

  • ✓ Power of Attorney: Authorising representatives in Nigeria to manage properties, bank accounts, or sign documents on your behalf.
  • ✓ Consent Letters: Allowing a minor to travel abroad when accompanied by only one parent or a guardian.
  • ✓ Affidavits & Statutory Declarations: sworne statements, change of name deeds, or age declarations.
  • ✓ Birth & Marriage Certificates: Validating foreign registrations for official use in Nigeria.
  • ✓ Academic Documents: Certifying diplomas and transcripts for employment or school admissions.
  • ✓ Corporate Records: Legally validating certificates of incorporation or board resolutions for overseas companies.

Affidavits & Statutory Declarations

An affidavit is a written statement of facts voluntarily made by an affiant under an oath administered by a person authorized by law. Commonly required affidavits for Nigerians in the UK include:

  • → Affidavit of Change of Name: Required if your name on different documents does not match, especially for passport or bank account updates.
  • → Affidavit of Marital Status: Declaring bachelorhood, spinsterhood, or divorce, often required for registering marriages.
  • → Statutory Declaration of Age: Used when a birth certificate is unavailable or needs correction.

We work with registered commissioners for oaths and notary publics to legally draft, swear, and notarise your affidavits, ensuring they are valid and recognized by Nigerian authorities.

Birth Certificate Legalisation & Attestation

For children born in the UK or Germany to Nigerian parents, the local birth certificate is not automatically accepted by Nigerian agencies like the NIS or banks. Similarly, adults who need a replacement or certified copy of their National Population Commission (NPC) birth certificate can process it directly.

For Adults (18+ Attestation / Re-issuance):
  • ✓ Sworn affidavit for attestation of birth from the high court
  • ✓ Passport photographs of the owner
  • ✓ Valid ID card (Passport, Driver’s License)
  • ✓ Full details of birth: Place of birth (LGA, State), Date of Birth, Parents' full names
  • ✓ Completed NPC application form (provided during appointment)
For Children & Minors (New Registration):
  • ✓ Child's original birth certificate from the country of birth (e.g., UK birth certificate)
  • ✓ Parent's valid Nigerian international passport
  • ✓ Proof of parent's address in the UK/Germany
  • ✓ Completed NPC application form (provided during appointment)

⚠️ Original Documents Required

Please bring the physical original copies of all required documents to your appointment for verification. Soft copies or photocopies are not accepted for official attestation stamps.

The 3-Step Legalisation Process

To successfully legalise a UK document for use in Nigeria, it must pass through three distinct regulatory layers:

  1. Step 1: Notarisation

    A certified Notary Public verifies your identity and signs the document, confirming it was executed legally in their presence.

  2. Step 2: FCDO Apostille

    The UK Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) attaches an official Apostille certificate to verify the credentials of the notary public.

  3. Step 3: Embassy Attestation

    Finally, the document is submitted to the Nigerian High Commission in London, where it is stamped and recorded, making it fully valid in Nigeria.
