Errors on Your NIN? How to Fix Name, DOB and Other Mistakes from the UK

A wrong name spelling, incorrect date of birth or outdated phone number on your NIN record can block your passport application, BVN linkage and more. Here is exactly how to fix it from the UK — no Nigeria trip required.

Why NIN Errors Matter

Your NIN is a permanent, lifelong record stored in NIMC's national identity database. It is the foundation for your passport, your BVN, your SIM registration and your access to virtually every Nigerian government service. An error on it does not just cause minor inconvenience — it can block critical processes:

  • ✗ Passport applications can be rejected when your NIN name doesn't match your passport name
  • ✗ BVN–NIN linkage can fail if the date of birth on your NIN doesn't match your bank's records
  • ✗ NIMC's self-service portal (*346#) may not return your NIN if the registered phone number is wrong
  • ✗ Government benefit programmes and financial services verify against the NIN database — discrepancies cause blocks

The good news: NIN errors can be corrected. The process is called a NIN modification and NIN-BVN Services handles it from the UK.

The Most Common NIN Errors

  • → Name misspelling — the most common issue. A missing letter, transposed characters, or an unofficial nickname captured during original enrolment
  • → Wrong date of birth — a day, month or year entered incorrectly at original enrolment, often because the enrolment officer typed it manually
  • → Wrong phone number — an old or mistyped number means NIN SMS never arrives and *346# doesn't work
  • → Outdated address — not a critical blocker but worth updating for accuracy
  • → Gender recorded incorrectly — rare but happens, particularly when the enrolment was done very quickly
  • → Wrong middle name or missing middle name — causes passport name mismatch
  • → Duplicate biometric record — most complex case, where two NINs exist for the same person from different enrolments

How to Fix NIN Errors in the UK

NIN modifications are processed through NIMC's modification portal via FUWA Agribusiness, NIN-BVN Services's licensed NIMC partner. You do not need to travel to Nigeria at any stage.

  1. Contact us first

    Call or WhatsApp +447459 788558. Tell us exactly what the error is and what your NIN or Transaction ID number is. We'll confirm what documents you need for your specific correction type before you book.

  2. Gather your supporting documents

    Different errors require different evidence. A name correction requires your passport and (for married name changes) a marriage certificate or deed poll. A DOB correction requires your birth certificate. We guide you on exactly what to bring.

  3. Book your modification appointment

    Modification appointments take 30–45 minutes — slightly longer than a new enrolment. Book at least 48 hours in advance so we can prepare the right forms.

  4. Attend your appointment

    Our officer verifies your documents, confirms the error, and submits the modification request through the FUWA Agribusiness NIMC portal. You receive a modification reference number as confirmation.

  5. Correction processed by NIMC

    NIMC processes modifications in the background. Changes are typically reflected in the database within 3–10 working days. We follow up on your behalf if there are delays.

Name Changes and Deed Poll

If you changed your name by deed poll after your NIN was issued — for example, following marriage, divorce, or a legal name change — your NIN record can be updated to reflect the new name.

  • → Bring your UK Deed Poll document (enrolled or unenrolled — both are accepted)
  • → Also bring your Nigerian passport (which may still show your old name) and Birth Certificate
  • → If your passport is also due for renewal with the new name, sort your NIN modification first — then renew your passport using the updated NIN

⚠️ Tip: Fix NIN Before Renewing Your Passport

If both your NIN and passport have an old or incorrect name, always correct the NIN first. Your passport renewal application checks against the NIN database. If the names don't match, the application gets flagged.

How Long Does NIN Modification Take?

  • → Appointment: 30–45 minutes on the day
  • → Modification reference number: issued on the day
  • → NIMC processing: 3–10 working days for standard corrections
  • → Complex cases (duplicate biometrics): may take longer — we follow up with NIMC until resolved
